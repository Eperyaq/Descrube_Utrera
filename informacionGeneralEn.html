Utrera is one of the most important cities in the province, due to its wealth, its past, and its heritage. Located 24 km from the capital, even before entering it, the importance of its buildings surprises; and upon entering, its manor houses, churches, and convents are surprising. Among the most notable monuments are the Castle, the Parish Church of Santiago el Mayor, the Parish Church of Santa María de la Mesa, the Sanctuary of Consolation, the Convent of the Purísima Concepción, the House of Arias Saavedra, the Riarola House, or El Niño Perdido. In its municipal area is the Natural Reserve of the Utrera Endorheic Complex, formed by the lagoons of Alcaparrosa, Arjona, and Zarracatín, and of great importance due to its marsh vegetation and avifauna.

History

The first settlements date back to prehistoric times. Numerous remains from the Neolithic and the Iberian period have been found. During the Roman period, Pliny placed it among the most notable in Baetica. It was called Castro Vinaria. Although no remains of the Roman city have been found, the magnitude of its necropolis gives us an idea of the importance of the city. After the Christian conquest, carried out by King Fernando III, in 1253 Alfonso X distributed the lands, building a walled fortress. It fell again into the hands of the Muslims in the year 1340. It was definitively conquered by the Infante Don Juan Manuel in the times of Alfonso XI. It would be destroyed by Mohamed V in the year 1368. Enrique II had to increase its privileges to facilitate its repopulation. After the War of Granada, during the 16th and 17th centuries, it underwent great urban development. The War of Independence was a hard blow. Its recovery would be slow throughout the 19th century.

Climate

It enjoys clear skies for most of the year, which provide a large number of hours of sunshine. The climatic type of the area is Mediterranean of the subtropical type —also classified as mesothermal subhumid—, and is characterized by hot and dry summers, and humid and temperate winters. The dry season, centered in the summer season, usually lasts more than four months. 41% of the rainfall occurs in autumn. The average annual temperature of the area is 17.5 °C. The absolute maximums have an average of 41.9 °C, being the hottest month July, where an average of 41.2 °C is reached. January is the coldest month of the year, and the average of its absolute minimum temperatures is 0.8 °C. The prevailing winds during the months of January and February are northeast. During the rest of the year, southwest winds stand out, especially between the months of May and August.
